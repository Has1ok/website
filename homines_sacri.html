homines sacri

mixed media installation with video mapping, silicone-covered objects and sound-scape created with recorded voices of children, who escaped Mariupol and Severodvinsk.

The term homo sacer is used by Agamben to refer to the state of exclusion, when a citizen loses their rights and autonomy, including the right to vote, the right to self-representation, and the right to control their own life. “Naked life”, to which a person is reduced, does not evoke sympathy and understanding until it finds itself in the spotlight of the media. The media can grant or deprive the “naked life” voice, but this voice is not autonomous, this voice must speak the language of those in power in order to be heard. The installation uses stones coated with silicone. The stone is both a tool and a weapon, a weapon of punishment for the excluded.
